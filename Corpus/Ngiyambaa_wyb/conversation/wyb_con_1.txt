# comments:		Donaldson (1977), p. 361 makes the following comments about the texts given in her Appendix: ``The reader should be cautious in drawing conclusions about the structuring of Ngiyamba: discourse from any of the texts, and particularly from Text 1, to which two speakers contributed. Each text represents a conscious effort to tell a story in Ngiyamba:, which has been almost wholly supplanted by English in the everyday lives of the narrators. There was often considerable discussion in English both before and during the telling, while narrators worked out what to say, listened to the recording of what they had said so far, and suchlike.'' Furthermore, about the glossing it should be noted that ``when the same morphemes in the same sequence in adjecent sentences, morpheme-by-morpheme glosses are omitted in all but the first sentence, [...]'' (Donaldson, 1977, p. 361). The original text gives morpheme segmentations with clitics <=> and inflectional affixes <-> indicated. These segmentations are given in <segmentation>. The orthography in <line_x> is derived by us by just removing these segmentations.
